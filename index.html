The Challenge: Food Security & Climate Change

Community gardens have emerged as a powerful solution; offering fresh produce and shared purpose. But now, climate change is shifting weather patterns, draining water supplies, and damaging soil, making it hard for these gardens to survive let alone thrive.

In South Africa, more than 12 million people face food insecurity every single day, highlighting the urgent need for sustainable solutions.

Case Studies & Success Stories

Soweto Urban Garden

Gauteng Province, South Africa

Tool banks and workshops offer hands-on training to the youth and locals, creating sustainable food sources and skills development.

Ngqeleni Village Soil Restoration

Eastern Cape, South Africa

Promotion of composting, manure use, and crop rotation to restore soil health and improve agricultural yields.

Kibera Slum Gardens

Nairobi, Kenya

Innovative techniques like sack gardening and hydroponics are used to grow kale and spinach. Community groups offer training and support.
